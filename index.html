European Foreign Currency Option Pricer

This interactive webpage prices European-style call and put options on foreign currency using the Garman-Kohlhagen model, which is the Black-Scholes model adapted for currency options. Time to maturity is entered in days and automatically converted into years using a 365-day convention.

Inputs

A call gives the right to buy foreign currency; a put gives the right to sell it.
Domestic currency price of one unit of foreign currency.
Exercise price in domestic currency per unit of foreign currency.
Example: 30, 90, 180, or 365 days.
Enter as a decimal, so 5% = 0.05.
Enter as a decimal. This is like the continuous yield on the foreign currency.
Annualized volatility entered as a decimal, so 12% = 0.12.
Garman-Kohlhagen formulas:

C = S e-rfTN(d₁) - K e-rdTN(d₂)
P = K e-rdTN(-d₂) - S e-rfTN(-d₁)

d₁ = [ln(S/K) + (rd - rf + σ²/2)T] / [σ√T]
d₂ = d₁ - σ√T

with T = days / 365

Notes:
The model assumes European exercise, lognormal exchange-rate dynamics, constant volatility, and constant domestic and foreign interest rates. Rho to rd measures sensitivity to the domestic interest rate, while rho to rf measures sensitivity to the foreign interest rate.
